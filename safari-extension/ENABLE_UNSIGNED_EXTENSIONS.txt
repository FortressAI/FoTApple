
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
🔧 WHY SAFARI DOESN'T SEE THE EXTENSION
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ISSUE: The app is "ad-hoc signed" (development signature)
       Safari won't show it without a special setting enabled

SOLUTION: Enable "Allow Unsigned Extensions" in Safari

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
📋 STEP-BY-STEP FIX (2 minutes)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

PART 1: Enable Developer Menu
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

☐ 1. Open Safari

☐ 2. Click "Safari" in the menu bar (top left of screen)

☐ 3. Click "Preferences..." (or press ⌘,)

☐ 4. Click "Advanced" tab
     • It's the LAST tab on the right
     • Has a gear icon ⚙️

☐ 5. Scroll to the bottom of the Advanced tab

☐ 6. Check the box: ✅ "Show Develop menu in menu bar"
     • This adds a "Develop" menu to Safari

☐ 7. Close the Preferences window


PART 2: Allow Unsigned Extensions
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

☐ 8. Look at the menu bar at the top of your screen

☐ 9. You should now see a new menu called "Develop"
     • Between "Bookmarks" and "Window"

☐ 10. Click "Develop"

☐ 11. Look through the menu for "Allow Unsigned Extensions"

☐ 12. Click it to enable it (a checkmark ✓ will appear)


PART 3: Restart Safari
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

☐ 13. Quit Safari COMPLETELY
      • Press ⌘Q
      • OR: Safari menu → Quit Safari
      • Make sure all Safari windows close

☐ 14. Wait 2 seconds

☐ 15. Open Safari again


PART 4: Enable Extension
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

☐ 16. Safari → Preferences (⌘,)

☐ 17. Click "Extensions" tab

☐ 18. NOW you should see "QFOT Wallet" in the list!

☐ 19. Check the box next to "QFOT Wallet"

☐ 20. Select "Allow on Every Website"

☐ 21. Close preferences

☐ 22. Look for ⚛️ icon in Safari toolbar

☐ 23. Click it to use your wallet!

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
❓ WHY IS THIS NEEDED?
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Production apps: Signed with Apple Developer certificate ($99/year)
Development apps: Ad-hoc signed (free, for testing)

Safari protects users by hiding unsigned extensions.
The "Allow Unsigned Extensions" setting tells Safari:
  "I'm a developer, show me development extensions"

This is NORMAL for development.
Once you test it and want to release publicly, we'd sign it properly.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
🔒 IS THIS SAFE?
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

YES! This setting:
  ✅ Only affects YOUR Mac
  ✅ Only shows extensions you explicitly install
  ✅ Doesn't weaken Safari's security
  ✅ Is used by all Safari extension developers
  ✅ Can be disabled anytime
