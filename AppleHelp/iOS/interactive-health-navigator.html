Interactive Health Navigator - "When Do I Need Help?"

Personalized guidance for medical, mental health, legal, and educational concerns

🎯 Overview

The Interactive Health Navigator is a revolutionary feature in the Personal Health app that helps users determine when they need professional help and what type of help they need. Using a 10-question assessment, it provides personalized recommendations with urgency levels and specific next steps.

The 10-Question Assessment

Question 1: What's concerning you most right now?

Options: - Physical symptoms or pain - Emotional or mental wellbeing - Legal issue or incident - Learning or education challenge - Something else

AI Analysis: Routes to domain-specific questions

Physical Health Branch

Question 2: How severe are your symptoms? - Life-threatening or very severe → Emergency recommendation - Moderate to severe → Urgent care - Mild to moderate → Doctor's office - Minimal → Self-care suggestions

Question 3: How long have you had these symptoms? - Just started (< 2 hours) → Emergency if severe - Recent (< 1 week) → Doctor - Ongoing (> 1 week) → Doctor follow-up - Chronic (> 1 month) → Specialist

Question 4: Do any of these apply? - Chest pain, difficulty breathing → EMERGENCY - High fever, severe pain → URGENT - Persistent symptoms → DOCTOR - Manageable symptoms → MONITORING

Mental Health Branch

Question 2: How are you feeling right now? - Thoughts of harming yourself or others → CRISIS support (988) - Severe distress, can't function → Emergency mental health - Struggling, but managing → Therapist recommendation - Coping, but want support → Counseling resources

Question 3: How long have you felt this way? - Right now, sudden → Crisis hotline - Days to weeks → Therapist - Weeks to months → Psychiatrist + therapist - Ongoing management → Medication review

Question 4: Do you have support? - No one to talk to → Crisis line + community resources - Some support → Therapist + support groups - Good support → Maintain + professional help - Strong support → Therapist for optimization

Legal Issue Branch

Question 2: What type of legal matter? - Workplace incident (injury, harassment, discrimination) - Civil rights violation - Criminal matter - Family/domestic issue - Contract/consumer dispute

Question 3: How urgent is this? - Happening right now → Document immediately + call 911 if unsafe - Just happened (< 24 hours) → Document + consult attorney - Recent (< 1 week) → Attorney consultation - Ongoing → Attorney + documentation

Question 4: Do you have evidence? - Yes, documented → Attorney review - Some evidence → Document more + attorney - No evidence → Start documenting + consult attorney - Need help documenting → Use FoT Legal app

Education Challenge Branch

Question 2: Who is this for? - Yourself (adult learner) - Your child (K-12) - Your student (if you're a teacher)

Question 3: What's the challenge? - Learning difficulty → Educational psychologist - Behavioral concerns → School counselor - IEP/504 needed → School district assessment - Academic performance → Tutor + teacher conference

Question 4: Has this been evaluated before? - Yes, has IEP/504 → Review accommodations - Evaluation in progress → Follow up with school - Never evaluated → Request evaluation - Don't know → Schedule parent-teacher conference

Final Recommendations

Based on the 10 answers, the navigator provides:

1. Urgency Level

Level Meaning Action Timeframe
🚨 EMERGENCY Life-threatening Call 911 immediately
🔴 CRISIS Immediate danger Call 988 or crisis line now
🟠 URGENT Needs prompt attention Within 24 hours
🟡 IMPORTANT Should address soon Within 1 week
🟢 ROUTINE Non-urgent Schedule when convenient

2. Recommended Professional

  • Emergency Services: 911
  • Crisis Hotlines: 988, Crisis Text Line
  • Medical: Primary care, urgent care, ER, specialist
  • Mental Health: Therapist, psychiatrist, counselor
  • Legal: Attorney, legal aid, police report
  • Educational: Teacher, counselor, psychologist, IEP coordinator

3. Specific Next Steps

Example for Moderate Depression:

🟡 IMPORTANT - Address within 1 week

Recommended Professional: Licensed therapist or counselor

Specific Next Steps:
1. Call your insurance to find in-network therapists
2. Or use Psychology Today therapist finder
3. Schedule appointment (may take 2-4 weeks)
4. In the meantime:
   - Track your mood daily in this app
   - Practice self-care (sleep, nutrition, movement)
   - Reach out to trusted friends/family
5. If thoughts of self-harm arise, call 988 immediately

Crisis Support Always Available:
• 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline
• Text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line)

4. Self-Care While You Wait

  • Evidence-based suggestions
  • Relevant to their concern
  • Realistic and actionable
  • Not a replacement for professional help

5. Resources & Contacts

  • Phone numbers to call
  • Websites with more info
  • Local resources if available
  • FoT app features to use

Recommended: School psychologist evaluation + parent-teacher conference

Your son may have an undiagnosed learning disability (dyslexia, ADHD, etc.). Early intervention is key.

Next Steps:

🛡️ Safety & Ethics

What the Navigator Does

✅ Provides guidance based on symptoms/concerns
✅ Recommends appropriate professionals
✅ Gives urgency levels
✅ Connects to crisis resources
✅ Documents the assessment with receipt

What the Navigator Does NOT Do

❌ Diagnose medical conditions
❌ Replace professional judgment
❌ Provide medical/legal advice
❌ Make treatment decisions
❌ Override user's judgment

Safety Guardrails

  1. Emergency Override:
  2. Any mention of self-harm → Immediate 988/crisis resources
  3. Any life-threatening symptoms → Emergency services

  4. Any unsafe situation → 911

  5. Disclaimer Shown:

    "This guidance is educational, not medical/legal advice. In an emergency, call 911. For mental health crisis, call 988."

  6. User Control:

  7. Can skip navigator anytime
  8. Can call 911 directly from app
  9. Crisis support always visible
  10. No barriers to help
