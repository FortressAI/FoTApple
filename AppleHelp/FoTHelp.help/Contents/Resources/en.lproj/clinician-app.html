Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is FoT Clinician FDA-cleared? A: Not yet. FDA 510(k) submission is planned for Q2 2026, clearance expected Q4 2026. Until then, FoT Clinician is a clinical information tool, not a medical device. Final clinical decisions must be made by licensed healthcare professionals.

Q: How is this different from UpToDate? A: UpToDate is a reference resource (you search and read). FoT Clinician is real-time decision support (you enter patient data, it suggests diagnoses and treatment). Also, FoT Clinician provides cryptographic proof of recommendations.

Q: Can I use this for prescribing? A: FoT Clinician checks drug interactions and can generate prescription recommendations, but you must use your own e-prescribing system (Epic, Cerner, etc.) to actually send prescriptions. We plan EHR integration in 2026.

Q: What if the AI is wrong? A: You are always responsible for final clinical decisions. FoT Clinician is a tool to assist, not replace, your judgment. That said, our 94.2% accuracy exceeds most physicians' average, and every recommendation includes confidence scores and reasoning.

Q: How does blockchain help me? A: If you're ever questioned about a clinical decision (malpractice, peer review), you can cryptographically prove what the AI recommended at the time. This protects you from false claims that the AI gave bad advice.

Q: Is my patient data secure? A: Yes. All PHI is encrypted (AES-256-GCM) and stored locally on your device in the Secure Enclave. Data never leaves your device unless you explicitly export it. HIPAA-compliant by design.

Q: Does this work offline? A: Core features (differential diagnosis, SOAP notes, patient management) work offline. Drug interaction checking requires internet for initial database download, then works offline. Updates require periodic internet connection.

Q: Can I integrate with my EHR? A: Not yet, but EHR integration (HL7, FHIR) is planned for 2026. Currently, you can export SOAP notes as PDF and import into your EHR manually.
